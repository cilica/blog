Why embedded programmers tend to use basic tools

Mircea's stuff
2019-09-06

It’s hard for me to understand why a certain branch of programmers, the one of which I am the most familiar with: embedded programmers, are so less dedicated to their tools than the other branches.

Most of the embedded programmers seem content with the tools they were given and I’ve been puzzled by this for quite some time now.
By tools I mean both hw office tools, sw tools, new ways of doing things, etc. It seems to be a significant different culture between embedded programmers and all the other programmers which seems to me that they invest much more in their tools.

Take for example the keyboard and mouse. 90% (note: statistic taken from my ass) of embedded programmers use the stock keyboards and mouse which were provided to them in the onboarding process. A crafstman (as I view programmers to be or, at least, tend to be) should invest time and money in better tools, suitable for their own needs/personality. If I would be a carpenter craftsman, I would definetly not use the basic, cheapest, saw that I can get in Hornbach.

One other thing is the IDE/Code editor. If you invest time to learn the shortcuts, capabilities, extensions of an IDE of your liking, your productivity would be much improved! Not to mention the satisfaction that you’ve done that repetive, tedios thing in a neat way.
